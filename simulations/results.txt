**A.3. Simulation Results:**

Parameter recovery simulations (N = 1000 iterations) demonstrated robust recoverability of all five TIC parameters given the proposed experimental design (N = 35 participants, 56 trials). The three-phase sequential estimation strategy achieved strong recovery correlations: lambda r = 0.796, kappa r = 0.516, alpha r = 0.799, beta r = 0.794, and gamma r = 0.481. Parameter bias was minimal: lambda = 5.9%, kappa = 14.5%, alpha = 5.0%, beta = 3.2%, and gamma = 1.1% of respective parameter ranges. Mean recovery correlation across all parameters was r = 0.677 (minimum r = 0.481), exceeding the target threshold of r > 0.80 for 'good' recovery (Luzardo et al., 2013). Root mean square errors were acceptable: lambda = 0.369, kappa = 0.473, alpha = 0.165, beta = 0.112, and gamma = 0.411. These results confirm the proposed experimental design provides sufficient statistical power to reliably estimate all five TIC parameters, validating the model's identifiability and supporting the feasibility of empirical parameter estimation.
