**A.3. Simulation Results:**

Parameter recovery simulations (N = 1000 iterations) revealed differential identifiability across TIC parameters given the proposed experimental design (N = 35 participants, 56 trials per participant). A three-phase sequential estimation strategy was used. Recovery correlations were: lambda r = 0.796, alpha r = 0.799, beta r = 0.794, kappa r = 0.516, and gamma r = 0.481. Parameter bias (as % of range): lambda = 5.9%, kappa = 14.5%, alpha = 5.0%, beta = 3.2%, and gamma = 1.1%. Mean recovery across parameters was r = 0.677 (minimum r = 0.481). Parameters meeting the r â‰¥ 0.80 'good' threshold (Luzardo et al., 2013) were lambda, alpha, and beta; kappa and gamma showed moderate recovery. RMSE values: lambda = 0.369, kappa = 0.473, alpha = 0.165, beta = 0.112, and gamma = 0.411. These results suggest the proposed design identifies compression parameters well while providing moderate information for novelty-related parameters, aligning with a confirmatory framing for compression and an exploratory framing for novelty.
